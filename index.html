You land in Kona at 11:30 on a Thursday and three weeks later you need to be at a desk in Southeast Asia. In between: a fit, curious couple who want to hike until you sweat, drift over coral, learn how a place actually works, and share most of it with friends who are already on the islands.

Two ways to do three islands

Big Island, Maui and Kauaʻi are locked in. The only call left is how to play the back half: go all-in on the Kalalau backpack with your friends, or trade the trek for a world-class dive and Nā Pali from every angle.

Plan A · The Kalalau trek

Backpack the Nā Pali

Big Island · 5 Maui · 4 Kauaʻi · 7
  • Backpack 11 mi to a wild Nā Pali beach and camp 2–3 nights — with your friends
  • The single greatest "makes-you-sweat" adventure in Hawaiʻi
  • Your deepest friend time — out on the trail together
  • Still get the Haleakalā crater hike, the manta night & the Lānaʻi dive
Trade-off: the Tue/Thu blackwater dive is hard to fit. Choose this if the backpack is the dream.
Plan B · The explorer

Nā Pali, three ways

Big Island · 6 Maui · 4 Kauaʻi · 6
  • An extra Big Island day to add the world-class blackwater dive (Tue night)
  • See Nā Pali from the air (heli), the sea (raft) and on foot (the 13 km Hanakāpīʻai Falls day hike)
  • More variety — a Hawaiian cultural village + Wailua kayak + Waimea Canyon
  • No multi-night commitment — a real bed every night
Trade-off: a little less friend time (they backpack without you). Choose this to sample it all.

The honest call: both are the full three-island trip — it comes down to one choice. Plan A goes all-in on a single epic, the Kalalau backpack with your crew. Plan B trades that one commitment for breadth: the blackwater dive, Nā Pali from every angle, a cultural village and a kayak day — sleeping in a bed each night. Itching for true wilderness + max time with friends → A. Prefer variety, the dive, and no exposed multi-night trek → B.

CHAPTER I · YOU LAND HERE · WITH FRIENDS

Hawaiʻi

Moku Nui · the Big Island

The youngest, rawest island — where the earth is still being made. You land here, and your friends are already waiting. Lava by night, coffee by morning, and the ocean's gentlest giants after dark.

Kīlauea & Hawaiʻi Volcanoes NP

Hike across a frozen lava-lake floor at Kīlauea Iki; if the volcano is awake — and it has been, in fountaining episodes — watch the caldera glow red after dark. The best single day on the island.

CHAPTER II · THE MIDDLE · JUST THE TWO OF YOU

Maui

the Valley Isle · your middle chapter

Four days off on your own — a deliberate, romantic pause between the friend-filled bookends. Maui's gift is a sunrise volcano you hike across like another planet, and the best diving in the islands.

Top experiences · ranked for you

1

Haleakalā sunrise → crater traverse

Watch the sun rise above a sea of clouds at 3,000 m, then hike 18 km down through a Martian crater of cinder cones and silverswords. Your hardest, strangest, most unforgettable day on foot.

The three you asked for

Three moments you'll talk about forever

A helicopter, the mantas, and one truly world-class dive. Here's the exact version of each we'd book — and why.

For Addie ♡

The doors-off flight over Nā Pali

≈ $359–399 · 60 min · Kauaʻi

You said you wanted to see the Hawaiʻi a hike can't reach. This is it: the fluted green cliffs from above, the wettest crater on earth ringed by a hundred waterfalls, and Manawaiʻopuna — the "Jurassic Park" falls — which is literally impossible to reach on foot.

Doors off, harnessed in, wind in your face, at first light when the air is clearest. It's the single best aerial in the islands, and you're already on Kauaʻi for it.

We'd book: Jack Harter (doors-off, full 65 min) or Mauna Loa (private, intimate). Morning slot, with a backup day for weather.
If Kīlauea is erupting during your Big Island days, we'd switch to a volcano flight instead — we'll watch the USGS feed for you.
After dark, in the water

Floating with the mantas

≈ $130 · 2 hrs · Keauhou Bay · no scuba needed

This is the one almost no one forgets. You hold a lit board on the surface; the lights gather plankton; and giant mantas — three, four metres across, utterly harmless — rise out of the black to barrel-roll inches beneath you, mouths open, again and again.

It's the most reliable manta encounter on earth, five minutes from your friends' base, in the calmest water on the coast. You snorkel it — no tank, no depth, no pressure. Just wonder.

We'd book: a small-group, "green-list" operator (Sea Paradise), early in your Big Island stay so there's a free re-ride if the ocean's shy.
Only if it's truly world-class

One unforgettable dive

≈ $230 · Kona · open-water certified

You don't dive for the sake of diving — so we only put one truly singular dive on the table: Kona's blackwater "Pelagic Magic." Two miles offshore, over open ocean, you hang on a lit line and watch the deep's nightly migration drift up around you — translucent, glowing creatures most divers never see. It was invented here; almost nowhere else offers it.

And if Maui happens, Lānaʻi Cathedrals — sunlight through a lava-tube roof — is the gentler, stained-glass alternative.

We'd book: Jack's Diving Locker (Tue/Thu). Comfortable in the water? Do the blackwater. Prefer something calmer? The manta dive — kneeling on the sand, mantas overhead — is pure magic and easy.
Addie — about the water…

Here's the truth: the most magical things in this whole trip ask almost nothing of you. The mantas? That's a snorkel — you float and watch. The turtles at Tunnels? Waist-deep, glass-calm summer water. You're already a certified diver, so the door is open whenever you want it — but nothing here requires you to be brave. The ocean in Hawaiʻi in September is warm, clear, and gentle, and these animals are the calmest giants you'll ever meet. We'll go at exactly your pace. ♡

"Is there a Polynesian Cultural Center, like the one on Oʻahu?"

Honest answer: not really — the PCC is a one-of-a-kind Oʻahu attraction and nothing on your three islands matches its scale. But the closest, genuinely-worth-it cultural immersions are easy adds — and the best of them are on Kauaʻi:

Kauaʻi · ~$5 · our pick to add

Kamokila Hawaiian Village

The only walk-through traditional village on your route — thatched hale, Hawaiian games like spear-throwing and ʻulu maika, and outrigger paddling up the Wailua River. Hands-on, authentic, and almost free.

Kauaʻi · $10 garden / ~$140 lūʻau

Smith's Tropical Paradise

A 30-acre botanical garden plus a multi-island cultural pageant — the most "PCC-flavoured" experience on the neighbour islands. This is the lūʻau we'd slot into your Sep 11 evening.

Big Island · already in your plan ✓

Puʻuhonua o Hōnaunau

The real thing, not a show: a sacred place of refuge with live koa-canoe carving, lauhala weaving and lei-making by Native Hawaiian practitioners. Deeper and more genuine than any village attraction — and you're already going.
